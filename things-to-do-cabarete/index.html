🪁 #1 Activity

Kitesurfing in Cabarete

💨 250–300 kitable days/year 💰 $75–$95/hr lessons ✅ All skill levels 🌡️ No wetsuit needed

Cabarete is the undisputed kitesurfing capital of the Caribbean — and it's not even close. The Alizé trade winds blow from the northeast almost every afternoon with clockwork consistency. Add a protective reef that flattens the inner water, a wide sandy beach for safe launches, and more IKO-certified instructors per square mile than anywhere else in the region, and you have the perfect storm (the good kind).

Whether you've never touched a kite or you're chasing freestyle tricks, Cabarete delivers. Beginners average about 9 hours to reach independent riding — and most say they're hooked after the very first session. The wind typically builds from noon to 4 PM, leaving your mornings free for yoga, caves, or a surf at Encuentro before the afternoon show begins.

🎯 Insider Tips
  • Wind picks up reliably between 12:30 and 1:30 PM — no need to rush breakfast
  • La Boca (the river mouth) is flat-water paradise — better for learning new tricks
  • Most schools include equipment; no need to ship your own gear
  • Private lessons cost more but get you riding up to 3x faster than group sessions
  • Oldest student at Kite Point was 72 — truly no age barrier
  • June–August is peak wind season; December–April is peak travel season

The Best Kite Spots in Cabarete

🏖️

Kite Beach — The Main Arena

Cabarete's most iconic spot. Wide sandy beach for safe launches, reef-protected inner water, flat water on the inside and waves on the reef outside. Gets crowded during peak season — arrive early or aim for morning sessions when the beach is quieter.

All Levels
🌊

La Boca — Flat Water Freestyle Paradise

Where the river meets the sea, creating a naturally flat freshwater lagoon east of town. The go-to spot for pro freestyle riders training new tricks. Small and sometimes crowded — best for more experienced kiters. A downwinder from La Boca back to Kite Beach is one of Cabarete's great rides.

Intermediate+
🌬️

Cabarete Bay — The Learning Grounds

The main bay in front of downtown Cabarete. Slightly choppier than Kite Beach but with plenty of open space and most of the schools. Great social spot — beach bars right there, instructors everywhere, and a buzzing kiting community.

Beginner-Friendly
🌋

Bozo Beach / Punta Goleta — Uncrowded Alternative

About 5 minutes from downtown, Bozo Beach has several kite schools and significantly fewer people. Shore break can be challenging especially in winter. A solid option when Kite Beach feels too packed.

All Levels

🏄 Surf Culture

Surfing at Playa Encuentro

📍 10-min drive from town 📅 350 rideable days/year 🏄 All skill levels

Playa Encuentro is the North Coast's premier surf break — and a genuine world-class wave. The inner reef serves up forgiving beginners' waves while the outer break can produce powerful, punishing swells during winter months. It's the kind of beach that gets better the more you visit it.

The rhythm at Encuentro is perfectly tuned for Cabarete's lifestyle: surfers own the beach from dawn until around noon, when the wind picks up and the kiters take over. Hit it early. The morning light on those waves is something special, and the breakfast spots right on the beach feed you before you paddle out.

🌊 Surf Tips
  • Go before 11 AM — wind picks up midday and makes conditions choppy
  • Chino Surf School on the beach rents boards and gives beginner lessons
  • Winter (Oct–Apr) brings bigger, more powerful waves — better for intermediates+
  • El Encuentro Surf Lodge has breakfast and great people-watching from the deck
  • Watch the outer reef from shore before paddling out — respect the local lineup

Scuba Diving — Sosúa (15 min away)

Dive Cabarete in Sosúa is the go-to PADI operator on the North Coast. The Canyon reef site is legendary — morays, turtles, and vibrant coral. Discovery dives available for non-certified divers; your instructor holds your hand the whole way (literally). Combines perfectly with a Sosúa beach afternoon.

All Levels~$80–$120
🏄‍♀️

Stand-Up Paddleboarding (SUP)

Morning is the golden window — before the afternoon wind makes it challenging. Cabarete Bay is glassy and calm until noon, making it ideal for SUP. Several schools along the beach rent boards. Great for families and absolute beginners. Exploring the mangroves near La Boca by SUP is a memorable experience.

Beginner~$20–$40/hr
🪂

Wing Foiling — The New Wave

The fastest-growing water sport in Cabarete. A hybrid of kitesurfing, windsurfing, and foiling — you hold a small wing and ride a foil board that lifts above the water. Oddly mesmerizing to watch from the beach. Several schools now offer wing lessons; no kite experience required.

All Levels$80–$100/hr
🚣

Kayaking & River Exploration — La Boca

The river mouth at La Boca is a completely different Cabarete from the beach strip — lush mangroves, river shacks serving fresh fish, fishermen casting nets, and tropical birds. Rent a kayak and explore at your own pace. Quiet, local, and a perfect morning activity.

Easy~$15–$25/hr
🎣

Snorkeling & Boat Tours

On calm days, snorkeling directly off Cabarete Beach reveals coral and colorful reef fish. For the best snorkeling on the North Coast, combine a boat tour with a Sosúa beach visit — the crystal-clear turquoise waters are exceptional. Ask your hotel to connect you with a trusted local operator.

Easy$20–$60
🌿 Nature & Adventure

El Choco National Park & The Cabarete Caves

🚶 15-min walk from town 💰 ~$20 guided tour 🦇 4 swimmable caves 🏛️ Taíno Indian history

While the rest of the Caribbean is doing pool days, Cabarete visitors are swimming in underground cenote-like caves carved by millions of years of limestone erosion, beneath a 77-square-kilometer tropical national park that begins less than a mile from the beach.

El Choco National Park — declared protected in 1976 — harbors over 200 caves, though 4 are safely swimmable and open to visitors. The limestone chambers feature stalactites, stalagmites, and in one remarkable cave, "musical stalactites" that ring like bells when tapped. The caves were once home to the island's original Taíno inhabitants, and their presence can still be felt in the ancient rock art and sacred chambers deep inside.

The tour is an 800-meter walk through the cave system, culminating in a freshwater underground pool where you can actually swim — one of the most surreal experiences in the Caribbean. Bring a swimsuit, wear comfortable shoes, expect some steep climbs (helmets and flashlights provided), and prepare for the bats that call the Cueva de los Murciélagos home. Hundreds of them. It's actually quite wonderful.

⚠️ Good to Know: Some cave sections require climbing steep steps and ladders with limited lighting. Active and able-bodied visitors will find it thrilling; those with mobility limitations should ask the guide beforehand. Bring insect repellent — mosquitoes are enthusiastic at the entrance. Cash only ($20 USD or pesos).

📍 How to Get There
  • Walk: Follow Callejón de la Loma road across from Ocean Dream Plaza — 15-min walk
  • Moto: 4 minutes by motoconcho, about $1–2
  • Tours: Organized excursions available from your hotel or local operators
  • Hours: Typically 9 AM – 5 PM; confirm locally before visiting
✈️ Beyond Cabarete

Best Day Trips from Cabarete

Cabarete's North Coast location puts some of the Dominican Republic's most spectacular natural attractions within easy reach. These are the day trips worth building your itinerary around.

💧

27 Damajagua Waterfalls

⏱️ ~1 hour from Cabarete

One of the Dominican Republic's most spectacular natural attractions. Trek through jungle, then jump, slide, and swim down 27 natural limestone cascades and turquoise pools. You choose how many falls to do — most first-timers aim for the top 12, which includes the most dramatic slides and jumps. Guides are mandatory and handle your safety equipment.

🧢 Helmets & life vest provided 💰 ~$30–$60 with guide 👨‍👩‍👧 Family-friendly (older kids)
🏝️

Cayo Arena — Paradise Island

⏱️ ~2 hours from Cabarete

A tiny, remote sandbar island off the northwest coast — turquoise water so clear you can see the bottom at 15 feet, surrounded by coral reefs bursting with marine life. Boat excursions include snorkeling gear. The kind of place that makes you question every life decision that kept you away from here. One of the Caribbean's genuinely great secret beaches.

🤿 Snorkeling included 💰 ~$60–$80 with boat 🌅 Full day
🏖️

Sosúa Beach & Diving

⏱️ 15 minutes from Cabarete

Cabarete's neighbor to the west. A calmer, more sheltered bay with crystal-clear turquoise water perfect for snorkeling right off the beach. Dive Cabarete operates here for PADI scuba. Beach bars, fresh coco locos, and a more relaxed vibe than Cabarete's action-packed strip — ideal for a recovery day.

🤿 Best diving on North Coast 💰 Free entry to beach 🚌 $1 by guagua
🌊

Playa Grande

⏱️ ~1.5 hours from Cabarete

One of the most visually stunning beaches in the entire Dominican Republic — dramatic white cliffs, crystal blue water, and notably fewer crowds than the main tourist zones. Considered by many to be the North Coast's most beautiful beach. Fresh fish lunch, cold Presidente, and a perfect day. Worth every minute of the drive.

🏖️ Stunning scenery 💰 Very affordable 🐟 Fresh seafood on-site
🚡

Puerto Plata & Teleférico

⏱️ 40–50 minutes from Cabarete

Puerto Plata is the region's main city. The big draw is the teleférico cable car up Mount Isabel de Torres — panoramic views of the entire north coast, a Christ the Redeemer statue at the summit, and jungle pathways to explore. Also worth visiting: the Victorian-era Malecón, the Amber Museum, and Fort San Felipe.

🚡 Cable car experience 💰 ~$10 teleférico ticket 🏛️ Colonial history
🌿

Laguna Gri Gri — Rio San Juan

⏱️ ~45 minutes east

A freshwater lagoon that connects to the ocean through mangrove tunnels. Boat excursions weave through the jungle-like waterways, visiting a natural pool carved into rock cliffs and secluded beaches accessible only by water. Excellent for bird watching, photography, and anyone wanting to experience the DR's wild interior from the water.

🚤 Boat excursion 💰 ~$20–$30 🐦 Bird watching
🐎 Unique Experience

Horseback Riding on Cabarete Beach

🌅 Sunset ride recommended 💰 ~$40–$80/person 🐴 No experience needed

There's something about riding a horse along a Caribbean beach at golden hour that registers differently than other travel experiences. The kind of memory that doesn't fade. Cabarete offers both morning beach rides and the decidedly superior sunset version — and the sunset ride, with the sky turning orange and pink over the ocean, is one of the most cinematic moments you'll find in the Dominican Republic.

Routes typically combine beach riding with coastal forest trails. Local operators are experienced, gentle with beginners, and the horses are well cared for. No riding experience required. Rides typically last 1–2 hours.

🌅 Pro Tips
  • Book the sunset ride — cooler, golden light, and far fewer beach flies than morning
  • Avoid early morning rides — flies can bother both you and the horses
  • Wear closed-toe shoes and comfortable pants if possible
  • Ask your hotel or local tour operators to connect you with trusted stables
  • Combine with a beachfront dinner afterward — perfect evening
🧘 Mind & Body

Wellness & Yoga in Cabarete

Cabarete's wellness scene is genuine, not performative. A real community of instructors, healers, and practitioners has built something here — and the combination of ocean air, tropical fruit, and daily movement makes healthy living feel effortless.

🧘 Yoga

Beachfront Yoga

Morning yoga on the beach or at your hotel is standard practice here. The Yoga Loft at eXtreme Hotel, Natura Cabana, and the Ocean Club Marriott all offer classes. Sunrise sessions with the sound of waves and ocean breeze are as good as it gets.

Daily Sessions Available
🍫 Cultural

Cacao Experience

Vivelo Experiences offers a hands-on cacao tour where you learn the history and process of Dominican chocolate production from bean to bar. Educational, delicious, and a unique window into one of the DR's most important crops.

~2-3 Hours · All Ages
💃 Dance

Bachata Dance Lessons

The Latin Dance Studio Cabarete offers private bachata and merengue lessons — the owner is a professional dancer who has mastered 20+ styles and comes to your accommodation. An hour of bachata changes how you hear music everywhere you go.

WhatsApp: +1 829-830-4330
💆 Spa

Natura Cabana Spa

Cabarete's finest wellness retreat. The spa at Natura Cabana Boutique Hotel uses local ingredients and ancient techniques to deliver treatments that feel genuinely transformative. Non-guests can book services. Pair with morning yoga and breakfast for the complete experience.

Eco-Luxury · Ocean-Side
🌙 After Dark

Cabarete Nightlife Guide

When the kites come down, the real Cabarete begins. The beachfront lights up, the bachata and reggaeton drift across the sand, and an international crowd of athletes, expats, and travelers finds its way to one of the Caribbean's most authentic nightlife strips. There's no dress code and no attitude — just a good time.

🍹

Mojito Bar — Every Night

The soul of Cabarete's beachfront. Best prices on the strip, incredible mojitos made with fresh mint and local rum, healthy food, organic juices, and a crowd that makes every evening feel like a reunion. Get there early for a beach table.

⭐ 4.4 · 806 Reviews
🎧

Bahia Bazaar / Ojo — Wed & Thu

Starts as a sunset cocktail spot and slowly transforms as the DJ warms up. By 10 PM it's a legitimate beach party. Tuesdays and Thursdays are programmed nights — check their Instagram stories for weekly lineups before you go.

🔥 Best Nightlife Spot
💃

LAX — Latin Night Thursdays

Thursday is the night. Arrive around 11:30 PM for bachata and merengue with a crowd of locals and long-timers who actually know how to dance. One of the most authentic Latin dance experiences on the North Coast. Even non-dancers end up on the floor.

🎵 Thursday Only
🎸

Pomodoro — Open Mic Sundays

Sunday evenings, Pomodoro transforms from an Italian pizza joint into a community open mic stage that draws musicians from across the globe. Guitars, horns, impromptu jams. Order a 2-for-1 pizza, grab a gin and tonic, and enjoy the show from the wide beachfront seating.

🍕 Every Sunday
🛍️

Wednesday Night Market

Every Wednesday from 4 to 10 PM on Cabarete Beach. Handcrafted jewelry, Dominican textiles, local art, street food, and live music. Even if you don't buy anything, the atmosphere is worth showing up for — locals, expats, and tourists all mixing in the best way.

📅 4–10 PM Wednesdays
🥂

Bliss Restaurant — Special Occasions

When you need the evening to be perfect. Oceanfront fine dining, impeccable service, locally-sourced seafood, prime meats, and a cocktail list that takes it seriously. Widely considered Cabarete's best restaurant — make a reservation.

🌟 Reserve Ahead
🎱

Don Pepe's Pool Hall — Tuesdays

A perfectly local Tuesday — 2nd floor above Helados Bon in downtown, tables are free (except the first one), a cold Bohemia grande costs 150 pesos, and the Dominican regulars will school you in pool without breaking a sweat. Authentic, affordable, hilarious.

🇩🇴 Locals' Spot · Tuesday
🎤

VoyVoy — Karaoke Mondays

Monday nights at VoyVoy bring out the best (and worst) voices in Cabarete. Open-air setup, surfer-kiter crowd, and an anything-goes energy that makes it the most fun you can have on a Monday anywhere on the Caribbean coast.

🎶 Monday Nights
