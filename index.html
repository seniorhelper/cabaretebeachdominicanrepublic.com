📍 Quick Answer — What is Cabarete Beach?

Cabarete Beach is a world-famous beach town on the Dominican Republic's North Coast, widely recognized as the Caribbean's premier kitesurfing and windsurfing destination. Located just 40 minutes east of Puerto Plata's international airport, Cabarete attracts travelers from over 50 countries with its near-constant trade winds (250–300 kitable days per year), vibrant beachfront restaurant scene, underground caves, tropical nightlife, and a welcoming expat and adventure sports community unlike anywhere else in the Caribbean.

🌴 About The Destination

Cabarete: The North Coast's Crown Jewel

Cabarete started as a quiet fishing village. Then, in the 1980s, adventure seekers discovered its secret: relentless northeasterly trade winds — known as the Alizé — blowing across a sheltered bay with a protective reef. By the 1990s, the world's top windsurfers and kitesurfers had made it their Caribbean home base, and Cabarete was officially on the map.

Today, it's so much more than a sports destination. Italian expats run oceanfront trattorias. Dominican fishermen still cast nets at dawn. Beach bars transform into bachata clubs after dark. El Choco National Park hides underground caves with freshwater lagoons just 10 minutes from the sand.

Things To Do in Cabarete

From the world's most consistent kite winds to hidden underground caves, Cabarete delivers more adventure per square mile than anywhere on the Caribbean coast.

⭐ #1 Activity

Kitesurfing & Windsurfing

With 250–300 kitable days per year and the legendary Alizé trade winds, Kite Beach is the undisputed capital of Caribbean wind sports. Over 10 IKO-certified schools offer lessons for complete beginners through advanced freestyle.

🌊 Adventure

Surfing at Playa Encuentro

Just minutes from downtown, Playa Encuentro is considered one of the Caribbean's finest surf breaks. Beginner-friendly inner sections, powerful outer reef waves, and multiple surf schools make it perfect for all skill levels.

🌿 Nature

El Choco National Park & Caves

Hidden beneath the tropical forest just outside of town, Las Cuevas de Cabarete are ancient limestone caves with crystal-clear underground lagoons you can actually swim in.

💧 Day Trip

27 Damajagua Waterfalls

An hour from Cabarete, 27 Charcos de Damajagua is one of the Dominican Republic's most extraordinary natural attractions. Trek through tropical forest, jump, slide, and swim down 27 natural cascade pools carved into turquoise-blue rock.

Horseback Riding on the Beach

Ride along Cabarete's shoreline at sunset — one of the most memorable Caribbean experiences you'll find anywhere. Local operators offer morning and sunset rides through coastal forest and along the beach.

🪁 Wind Sports Capital

Kitesurfing in Cabarete: The Full Picture

Ask any experienced kitesurfer where in the Caribbean they'd go if they could only pick one spot — and most will say Cabarete. The combination of the Alizé trade winds, a protective reef creating a flat-water lagoon at La Boca, and the open bay at Kite Beach creates near-perfect conditions for every level of rider.

Not a kitesurfer? Watching hundreds of kites fill the sky from a beachfront bar with a cold Presidente in hand is genuinely spectacular — the kind of scene you only find in Cabarete.

Cabarete Nightlife: The Beach Never Sleeps

When the kites come down, Cabarete transforms. The beachfront bars flip on their lights, the bachata and reggaeton start drifting across the sand, and a cosmopolitan crowd of expats, athletes, and travelers congregates for one of the Caribbean's most authentic nightlife experiences.

🍹

Mojito Bar

The quintessential Cabarete experience. Lowest prices on the beachfront, organic juices, incredible mojitos, and a crowd that spans every nationality. Open all day.
